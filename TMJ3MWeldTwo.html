Weld Test One

Material Preparation:
 ➢ Two steel pieces that would be welded together to make a T
 ➢ Lines were drawn with 1" spacing so that there's space for 12 welds (6 on each side)

Welding:
 ➢ Tack welds were done on both ends such that the two pieces actually made a T-joint.
 ➢ Welds were done on both sides in a loop-the-loop pattern so that they overlap

Finish:
 ➢ Cleaned welds up with wire brush

Comments: Weld Test Two, in my opinion was better than Weld Test One; I generally found it easier to aim into a corner than onto a flat plate.
