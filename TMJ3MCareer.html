Tool and die maker

Description: Tool and die makers make toos used in manufacturing, such as jigs, fixtures, dies, molds, machine tools, lathe tools, etc. They are work closely with manufacturing engineer and there is much turnover between jobs. They typically use manually controlled machine tools, but training in more computer oriented skills are also used to work with technologies sh=uch as CNCs and CAD.

Training: Most tool and die makers undergo an apprenticeship and recieve on the job training. It typically takes 8 000 hours (4 years) to become a tool and die maker with 7 280 of those hours spent as work experience and the other 720 as in-class learning. In Ontario, becoming a tool and die maker requires a certifying exam. When the certifying exam is passed, Skilled Trades Ontario will issue a Certificate of Qualification in this trade.
