Machinist's Hammer

Hammer Head: Material Preparation
 ➢ Steel hex shaft cut to 3 1⁄4"
 ➢ The ends were faced off at 315 rpm using the cutting tool.

Hammer Head: Diameter
 ➢ Both ends had their diameter turned down to 600 thou using the cutting tool. The turned down diameter is 1⁄2" in length.

Hammer Head: Chamfer
 ➢ Using the chamfering tool two chamfers were cut into the ends.

Hammer Head: Milling the Hole
 ➢ Clamped the hammer head perpendicular into the clamp on the mill.
 ➢ Drill straght down, slowly and not all at once, periodically applying oil.
Comments: My hole was not very straight since I didn't clamp the hammer head perfectly perpendicular the drill.


Hammer Head: Tapping the Hole  ➢ Clamped the hammer head with paper towel or regular paper around it (to prevent the clamp from leaving marks).
 ➢ Add the tapping oil.
 ➢ Start turning the tap into the hole, for the first couple turns watch carefully to ensure that the tap is perpendicular to the hammer head.
Comments: My hammer head soesn't sit square on my hammer handle on the y-axis and it's likely because I the tap wasn't quite square while I tapped my hammer head.

 ➢ Sanded the edges of the hammer head.

Hammer Handle: Material Preparation
 ➢ Aluminum ⌀ 3⁄4" cut to 8 3⁄4"
 ➢ The ends were faced off at 500 rpm using the cutting tool
 ➢ Both ends were center drilled

Hammer Handle: Diameter
 ➢ One end had a 1" long section's diameter turned down to 400 thou
 ➢ Another 1⁄2" was further turned down to 375 thou
 ➢ Both ends were center drilled
Comments: I messed up during this step as near the end while the lathe was still slowing down, I moved the tool away back leading to a sinusoidal looking pattern on that section.

Hammer Handle: Taper
 ➢ The cutting tool was set to 3°
 ➢ Then, the tool was manually moved until it the hammer handle had a nice taper that ended at 400 thou
Comments: It feels like the finish wasn't as nice as it could have been, since I could have gone slower while I was doing the final layer.


Hammer Handle: Diameter(2)
 ➢ Using the cutting tool, the diameter of the main hammer was turned down to 720 thou.

Hammer Handle: Square Shoulder
 ➢ The grooving tool was used to cut a square shoulder 1⁄2" in with the resulting radius being 280 thou
 ➢ 130 RPM
 ➢ Oil was periodically applied
Comments: I don't think I managed to get the grooving tool square, this was before I knew the trick of bringing the tool against the chuck to make sure that it's perpendicular.

Hammer Handle: Square Shoulder
 ➢ The chamfering tool was used in a couple of places (both ends and on the side of the shoulder next to the ends) to set them to an angle.

Hammer Handle: Threading on Lathe
 ➢ The threads were rough cut to ~33 thou on the lathe
 ➢ Autofeed was changed to be much slower
 ➢ Started the threading when the machine came to a certain thread on the lathe that it would lock in at
Comments: I don't think I could recite every step that it took to cut threads without looking them up (which is why they're not listed here). I feel that this was the most stressful part since if the threads were messed up a good chunck of the previous steps had to be re-done and it was as easy to mess-up as missing a number once and crossthreading.


Hammer Handle: Threading with Die
 ➢ To finish the threads a die was pressed onto the threads
Comments: I remember having to turn the die again outside the lathe since it wasn't quite done when I first took the die off.

Hammer Handle: Knurling
 ➢ Knurling tool was set up so that both the top and bottom of the wheel was touching the hammer handle
 ➢ 205 RPM
 ➢ Oil was periodically applied.
 ➢ When the knurling tool got to one end the gears were reversed so the tool would traverse backwards to the other end and so on and so forth; this was done so that the knurling tool didn't stop touching the hammer handle.
Comments: I went a bit to far, causing the wide end of the taper to also have knurling.

Hammer Handle: Groove
 ➢ The chamfering tool was used to cut a groove betwwen the kurling and the taper mostly for aesthetic reasons
Comments: Since the knurling crept up onto the taper, the groove also wasn't perfectly between the taper and the turned down diameter.
